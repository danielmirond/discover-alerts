**Biohacking** is the practice of managing your own biology through **science, technology and lifestyle changes** to optimize physical performance, mental clarity and longevity. It's built on a powerful idea: your body is a system that can be **"hacked"** or fine-tuned to operate at its full potential.

Unlike conventional medicine, which treats illness once it appears, biohacking is **proactive**: it aims to optimize biological function before something goes wrong, extend biological youth and maximize cognitive capacity.

## The 4 types of biohacking

Not all biohacking is the same. There are four main approaches, ranked from most accessible to most extreme:

### 1. Lifestyle and nutrition

The entry point. No devices, no supplements — just consistency. It includes:

- **Intermittent fasting** (16:8, 18:6, weekly 24h)
- **Sleep optimization** — consistent schedule, total darkness, cool temperature
- **Cold exposure** — cold showers, ice baths (cold plunge)
- **Meditation and stress management** — lowering chronic cortisol
- **Breathwork** — Wim Hof, box breathing, heart coherence

Free, backed by solid science and the effects compound over time.

### 2. Technology and biometrics

This is where **wearables** come in: smart rings (Oura), performance bands (WHOOP), watches (Apple Watch, Garmin) and continuous glucose monitors (CGM). They measure data like:

- Heart rate and **variability (HRV)**
- Sleep quality and stages
- Nighttime body temperature
- Real-time glucose levels
- Blood oxygen (SpO2)

The value isn't in the data itself but in using that information to **make decisions**: what to eat, when to train, how to manage stress.

### 3. Supplementation and nootropics

The strategic use of compounds — natural or synthetic — for specific goals:

- **Longevity**: NMN, NR, resveratrol, spermidine
- **Sleep**: magnesium glycinate, apigenin, L-theanine
- **Cognition**: creatine, omega-3, alpha-GPC, L-tyrosine
- **Inflammation**: curcumin, omega-3, alpha-lipoic acid

Golden rule: **supplementation does not compensate for a bad lifestyle**. Pillars 1 and 2 first.

### 4. Grinder biohacking

The most extreme and niche branch. It involves implanting electronic devices or sensors under the skin: RFID chips, finger magnets to "feel" magnetic fields, subcutaneous biosensors. It's not the focus of BiohackLab — here we concentrate on evidence-based, reversible interventions.

## The 4 fundamental pillars

Beyond categories, all serious biohacking is organized around four pillars:

### Sleep optimization

Sleep is the **master multiplier** of any intervention. Respecting circadian rhythms — morning sunlight exposure, complete darkness at night, cool bedroom temperature — is free and has the highest impact per dollar invested.

During deep sleep your body consolidates memory, repairs tissue, produces growth hormone and clears brain toxins via the glymphatic system. Poor sleep invalidates everything else.

### Nutritional biohacking

Beyond "eating healthy." It means adjusting intake based on individual needs, testing approaches like:

- **Intermittent fasting** for metabolic flexibility
- **Nutritional ketosis** for neuroprotection
- **Chrono-nutrition** — eating within the circadian window
- **Microbiome** — diverse fiber, fermented foods, polyphenols

### Longevity

Specific techniques to slow or reverse biological aging markers, popularized by experts like **David Sinclair** (Harvard) or **Peter Attia**:

- Sirtuin activation (NMN, NR, caloric restriction)
- Senolytics — removing senescent cells
- Biological age testing (TruAge, GlycanAge)
- Mitochondrial protection

### Environmental management

Controlling external factors that impact your biology:

- **Light** — block blue light at night, maximize morning sunlight
- **Air** — HEPA purifiers, plants, ventilation
- **Water** — filtration, mineralization
- **Temperature** — cold/heat contrast (sauna + cold plunge)
- **Electromagnetic fields** — minimize exposure during sleep

## Where to start

The temptation when discovering biohacking is to buy the Oura, join a gym, order 12 supplements and start fasting on Monday. **Don't do that.**

The path that works for most people:

1. **Week 1–2**: Set a consistent sleep schedule. 10 min of sunlight upon waking. Complete darkness after 10 PM.
2. **Week 3–4**: Add a 30-min daily outdoor walk. Cut caffeine after 2 PM.
3. **Month 2**: Experiment with 16:8 fasting (eating between noon and 8 PM).
4. **Month 3**: Add magnesium glycinate (400 mg pre-sleep) and omega-3.
5. **Month 4+**: Consider a wearable (Oura or WHOOP) only once fundamentals are established.

Each intervention builds on the previous. **Small habit changes before advanced technologies.**

## Biohacking vs. health fads

The difference between biohacking and a simple lifestyle change lies in three elements:

1. **Measurement** — decisions based on personal data, not general recommendations
2. **Experimentation** — test, measure, adjust (n=1)
3. **Scientific basis** — interventions backed by studies, not trends

It's not about buying things. It's about **understanding your biology and optimizing it with the right tools**.
