## Who is Augustinus Bader

Professor **Augustinus Bader** is not a beauty influencer. He's a German physician, Chair of Applied Stem Cell Biology at **University of Leipzig**. He has spent 30 years researching how to activate endogenous tissue regeneration mechanisms — primarily to treat severe burns and chronic wounds.

His medical patents (TFC8®) have saved the lives of children with severe burns in Germany since 2008. In 2018, he adapted the same technology to cosmetics. The Cream is the result.

## What TFC8® is

**Trigger Factor Complex 8** is the cocktail of molecular signals that makes Bader unique. It includes:

- **Specific amino acids** — the "messengers" between cells
- **Fat-soluble vitamins** in active form
- **Synthesized molecules** that mimic embryonic regeneration signals
- **Hyaluronic acid** of different molecular weights

The declared mechanism: activate **skin stem cells** (which exist in all of us but become less efficient with age) to accelerate tissue renewal, collagen production and skin barrier repair.

**Critical note**: the mechanism is well characterized in regenerative medicine (burns), but peer-reviewed studies specifically for cosmetic use are limited. Bader publishes internal data, not independent. That's why we classify it as **moderate evidence** — not strong.

## The honest question: is $295 justified?

Depends on your reference:

- **Vs La Mer / La Prairie ($500–700)**: yes, Bader has better scientific base and lower price
- **Vs OneSkin ($160)**: Bader has more complex technology but OneSkin has more peer-reviewed studies
- **Vs pharmacy retinol creams ($30–80)**: a CeraVe or Inkey List retinol gives you 80% of the result at 15% of the price

**Practical rule**: if your monthly cosmetic budget is under $60, start with retinol + niacinamide + SPF. If you can invest $100+/month, Bader is the most justifiable premium option on the market.
