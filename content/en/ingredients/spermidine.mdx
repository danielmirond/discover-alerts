If autophagy has a molecular switch, spermidine is the finger that flips it. This natural polyamine — present in every cell of your body and in fermented foods for millennia — has become one of the most studied compounds in the science of aging. And most remarkably: epidemiological data suggest that populations with higher spermidine intake live longer.

<EvidenceBadge level={2} label="Moderate evidence" />

## What is spermidine

Spermidine is a polyamine — an organic molecule with multiple amino groups — originally discovered in seminal fluid (hence the name, inelegant but accurate). It is present in all eukaryotic cells, where it plays essential roles in cell proliferation, DNA stability, and autophagy regulation.

Spermidine levels decline with age. A study by Madeo et al. showed that intracellular spermidine levels in 70-year-olds are approximately 30–40% lower than in 30-year-olds. This drop correlates with diminished autophagy — the cellular recycling system that clears damaged proteins, dysfunctional organelles, and intracellular pathogens.

## Mechanism: autophagy as a longevity engine

Spermidine activates autophagy through an elegant mechanism: it inhibits the enzyme **EP300** (an acetyltransferase), which induces the deacetylation of multiple ATG proteins (autophagy-related genes). The net effect is equivalent to caloric restriction, but without having to stop eating.

### Molecular pathway

1. **EP300 inhibition** → deacetylation of autophagic proteins
2. **Activation of ATG5, ATG7, Beclin-1** → autophagosome formation
3. **Fusion with lysosomes** → degradation of damaged cellular material
4. **Component recycling** → new amino acids and lipids made available

This process is particularly relevant for:
- **Neurons** — clearance of protein aggregates (tau, alpha-synuclein)
- **Cardiomyocytes** — mitochondrial cleanup, fibrosis prevention
- **Immune system** — renewal of senescent T cells

## Scientific evidence

### Foundational studies

**Eisenberg et al., 2009 (Nature Cell Biology):** First study demonstrating that spermidine extends lifespan in yeast, flies, worms, and human cells in culture. Mechanism: autophagy induction.

**Eisenberg et al., 2016 (Nature Medicine):** Spermidine in mice reduced age-related cardiac pathology and extended lifespan. Effect mediated by autophagy (confirmed with ATG5 knockouts).

### Epidemiological data in humans

**Bruneck Cohort (Austria), Kiechl et al. 2018 (American Journal of Clinical Nutrition):** Follow-up of 829 individuals over 20 years. Those in the top tertile of dietary spermidine intake had a **30% lower mortality risk** than those in the bottom tertile. The difference was equivalent to approximately 5 additional years of life.

**SmartAge Trial (2022):** Pilot trial in 30 older adults with subjective cognitive decline. 1.2mg/day of spermidine for 3 months improved memory performance and cognitive function.

## Natural sources

Spermidine is present in many foods, especially fermented and sprouted ones:

| Food | Spermidine (mg/kg) | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Wheat germ** | 243 | #1 source, basis of spermidineLIFE |
| **Natto (fermented soy)** | 56–80 | Basis of Primeadine |
| **Aged cheese (12+ months)** | 30–50 | Parmigiano, aged Manchego |
| **Mushrooms (shiitake, button)** | 10–20 | Accessible option |
| **Green peas** | 15–20 | Frozen retain levels |
| **Chicken liver** | 25–30 | For those who tolerate it |
| **Mango** | 10–15 | Fruit with the most spermidine |

A diet rich in these foods can provide 8–12mg of spermidine per day — significantly more than the standard supplementary dose of 1–2mg, which suggests that diet should be the first line of approach.

## Dosage and protocol

**Standard supplementary dose:** 1–2mg of spermidine/day
**Optimal timing:** in the morning, on an empty stomach (enhances the autophagy from the overnight fast)
**Duration:** minimum 3 months for measurable effects

### Synergy with intermittent fasting

Spermidine and fasting activate autophagy through complementary pathways. Taking spermidine at the end of the fasting window (before breaking the fast) maximizes the autophagic window.

## Safety

Spermidine is one of the supplements with the best safety profile:

- Present in the human diet since time immemorial (fermented foods, cheese, legumes)
- No serious adverse effects reported in any clinical trial
- Average European intake is 7–12mg/day through diet
- Supplementary doses (1–6mg) are well below typical dietary intake

**Caution:** individuals with wheat allergy should choose Primeadine (soy-based) instead of spermidineLIFE (wheat germ-based).
