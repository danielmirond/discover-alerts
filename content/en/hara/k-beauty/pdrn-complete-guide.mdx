In Korean dermatology, PDRN is not a new ingredient. It has spent a decade in Seoul clinics as a regenerative injectable — the so-called "salmon DNA injections" that K-pop celebrities mention without hesitation. What is new is its leap into topical skincare: brands like Medicube and VT Cosmetics have formulated OTC versions that promise regenerative benefits without the needle. The question is whether the science supports that promise.

<EvidenceBadge level={2} label="Moderate evidence" />

## What Is PDRN

PDRN (Polydeoxyribonucleotide) consists of low molecular weight DNA fragments (50-1,500 kDa) extracted from salmon sperm (*Oncorhynchus keta*). It is not functional DNA — it does not modify your genetics. These are fragments that act as biological signaling agents.

The original research is Italian, not Korean. In the 1990s, researchers at the University of Turin discovered that these DNA fragments accelerated the healing of chronic wounds, diabetic ulcers, and burns. The mechanism turned out to be elegant: PDRN activates the adenosine A2A receptor in fibroblasts.

### Mechanism of Action

The adenosine A2A receptor is a metabolic gateway. When PDRN activates it:

1. **Fibroblast proliferation** — the cells that produce collagen multiply
2. **Type I and III collagen synthesis** — direct increase in production
3. **Angiogenesis** — formation of new blood vessels (improved tissue nutrition)
4. **Anti-inflammatory effect** — reduction of TNF-alpha and IL-6
5. **Cell rescue** — protects cells under hypoxia or oxidative stress

This mechanism is well documented in the injectable context. The question for topical cosmetics is whether PDRN fragments penetrate the stratum corneum in sufficient quantity to activate dermal receptors.

## PDRN vs PN: The Market Confusion

The K-Beauty market uses "PDRN" and "PN" (polynucleotides) as if they were interchangeable. They are not:

| Concept | Molecular Weight | Source | Regulation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PDRN** | 50-1,500 kDa | Salmon sperm | Medical device (injectable) |
| **PN** (Polynucleotides) | Variable (broader range) | Salmon or other sources | Cosmetic (topical) |
| **HPT-DNA** | Variable | Salmon | Marketing term |

PDRN is a specific, standardized fraction of PN. When a serum says "PDRN" but is an OTC cosmetic, it technically contains PN, not pharmaceutical-grade PDRN. This does not mean it is ineffective — it means the terminology is imprecise.

## Scientific Evidence

### Injectable (Strong)

**[Shin et al., 2019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31249056/):** Meta-analysis of 14 clinical trials with injectable PDRN in wound healing. Significant reduction in healing time, improvement in regenerated tissue quality. Level of evidence: high.

**Kim et al., 2020 (Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology):** Intradermally injected PDRN improved periorbital wrinkles, elasticity, and hydration in 45 participants after 4 sessions. 32% improvement in elasticity measured with Cutometer.

### Topical (Emerging)

The evidence for topical PDRN/PN is more limited:

- In vitro studies show that salmon DNA fragments stimulate fibroblasts in culture
- A Korean pilot study (2021, not published in peer-review) reported improvement in texture and hydration after 8 weeks with a 2% PN serum
- Independent clinical data for topical formulations are scarce

The gap between injectable evidence (robust) and topical evidence (preliminary) is real. That said, the A2A mechanism is plausible for topical use if the formulation achieves adequate penetration.

## How to Incorporate PDRN Into Your Routine

### Position

1. **Cleansing** (double cleanse if you wear SPF/makeup)
2. **Toner** (hydrating)
3. **PDRN serum/ampoule** <-- here (low molecular weight actives first)
4. **Other actives** (vitamin C morning, retinol night)
5. **Moisturizer**
6. **SPF** (morning)

### Compatibility

- **Niacinamide** — compatible, documented synergy
- **Vitamin C** — compatible (use in the morning)
- **Retinol** — compatible (use at night, alternating days initially)
- **AHA/BHA** — separate by 15-20 minutes
- **Peptides** — compatible, complementary mechanisms
