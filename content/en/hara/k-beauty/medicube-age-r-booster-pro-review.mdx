## The Three Technologies

### Electroporation

Electroporation applies short-duration electrical pulses that create transient pores in the cell membrane. The result: serum actives penetrate 10-20 times more effectively than with manual application. The pores close within seconds, with no permanent damage.

This is different from iontophoresis (which uses direct current to move charged molecules). Electroporation works with molecules of any charge and size, including high molecular weight peptides that would not normally cross the stratum corneum.

### EMS (Electrical Muscle Stimulation)

Micro-currents that passively contract the facial muscles. The principle is simple: a muscle that contracts regularly maintains its tone. With age, the facial muscles (especially along the jawline and the zygomatic area) lose tone, contributing to sagging.

The evidence for facial EMS is moderate. A 2020 study in *Dermatologic Surgery* showed improvement in jawline firmness after 8 weeks of daily micro-current use. The effects are subtle and cumulative.

### LED (630nm Red + 470nm Blue)

- **Red (630nm):** stimulates collagen production in fibroblasts, reduces inflammation. Moderate-to-strong evidence in dermatological phototherapy.
- **Blue (470nm):** antibacterial activity against *Propionibacterium acnes*. Useful for mild-to-moderate acne.

## Our Test: 8 Weeks

### Weeks 1-2: Adaptation

The first session produces a mild tingling sensation from the EMS. Intensity is adjustable across 5 levels; we started at 2 and moved up to 3 in the second week. The recommended protocol: cleanse, apply water-based serum generously, pass the device for 10 minutes across facial zones (forehead, cheeks, jawline, neck).

Medicube recommends their Glow Boosting Pads (included), but the device works with any water-based serum: hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, vitamin C. Do not use with oils (they block electrical conduction).

### Weeks 3-4: First Changes

The skin absorbs serum noticeably better. After each session, the feeling of hydration is deeper and lasts longer than with manual application. This is the electroporation effect: more product reaches the dermis.

### Weeks 5-8: Evaluation

- **Texture:** perceptible improvement. Pores less visible, skin smoother to the touch
- **Jawline firmness:** subtle but real. It is not a lift — it is an improvement in tone
- **Radiance:** consistent, likely due to improved absorption of actives
- **Wrinkles:** no significant change in established lines. Fine periorbital lines softened slightly

### What It Does NOT Do

- It does not eliminate deep wrinkles
- It does not replace a professional radiofrequency treatment
- It does not deliver results without serum (the device is the vehicle, not the active)
- It does not function as a surgical facelift
