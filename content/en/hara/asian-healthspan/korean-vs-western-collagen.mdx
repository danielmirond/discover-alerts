The oral collagen market moves $7 billion a year. Korea and Japan have been consuming collagen for decades in formats the West is only beginning to discover: individual sticks, chewable jellies, drinkable ampoules, peach-flavored powders. The real question is not whether oral collagen works (there is evidence that it does), but whether the Asian approach offers genuine advantages over the Western one.

## The science of oral collagen

### Does it actually work?

Yes, with nuances. The evidence has improved significantly since 2019:

**[Proksch et al., 2014 (Skin Pharmacology and Physiology)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23949208/):** 69 women, 2.5g or 5g of collagen peptides for 8 weeks. Significant improvement in skin elasticity vs placebo. The improvement persisted 4 weeks after discontinuation.

**[Bolke et al., 2019 (Nutrients)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30681787/):** Review of 11 studies with 805 participants. Oral collagen improved skin hydration, elasticity, and density. Effective dose: 2.5-10g/day for a minimum of 8-12 weeks.

**[de Miranda et al., 2021 (International Journal of Dermatology)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33742704/):** Meta-analysis of 19 RCTs, 1,125 participants. Significant improvement in hydration and elasticity. Low molecular weight peptides showed better absorption.

### How it works

Collagen peptides are not absorbed "intact" to go directly to your skin. The mechanism is more sophisticated:

1. They are digested into di- and tripeptides (Pro-Hyp, Hyp-Gly)
2. These peptides enter the bloodstream
3. They accumulate in the dermis
4. They stimulate fibroblasts to produce new collagen of their own
5. The effect is signaling-based, not directly structural

## Korean collagen vs Western collagen

### The Korean approach

- **Predominant source:** marine (fish), especially cod and salmon
- **Molecular weight:** low (1,000-3,000 Da, nanopeptides)
- **Formulation:** combined with vitamin C, hyaluronic acid, elastin
- **Formats:** individual sticks, jellies, liquid ampoules, flavored powders
- **Philosophy:** "beauty from within," an ingestible as part of the skincare routine
- **Typical dose:** 1,000-3,000 mg/day

### The Western approach

- **Predominant source:** bovine (Vital Proteins) or marine
- **Molecular weight:** variable (2,000-10,000 Da)
- **Formulation:** pure collagen, minimal additives
- **Formats:** large powder tub, capsules
- **Philosophy:** sports / joint supplement
- **Typical dose:** 5,000-15,000 mg/day

### Which absorbs better?

Molecular weight matters for absorption. Smaller peptides cross the intestinal barrier more efficiently:

| Molecular weight | Absorption | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| 1,000-3,000 Da | Excellent | Korean/Japanese |
| 3,000-5,000 Da | Good | Premium Western |
| 5,000-10,000 Da | Moderate | Standard Western |

Korean peptides have a theoretical advantage in bioavailability, but the total dose is lower (1-3g vs 5-15g). The net result is probably similar: what matters is the total amount of dipeptides that reach the bloodstream.

## Protocol

- **Minimum effective dose:** 2.5g/day (Proksch 2014)
- **Optimal dose:** 5-10g/day
- **Minimum duration:** 8 weeks to see skin results
- **With vitamin C:** always (essential cofactor for collagen synthesis)
- **Timing:** morning on an empty stomach or before bed (both work)
- **Consistency:** benefits revert once you stop taking it
