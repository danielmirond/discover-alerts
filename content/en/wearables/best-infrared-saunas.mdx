## Why an infrared sauna?

Unlike traditional Finnish saunas (which heat the air), infrared saunas heat your body directly with infrared light. The result: **more comfortable ambient temperature (40–60°C / 104–140°F)** with similar or superior physiological benefits:

- **Cardiovascular health**: simulates passive cardio exercise, improves endothelial function
- **Detoxification**: deep sweating with elimination of heavy metals
- **Muscle recovery**: increases blood flow and reduces inflammation
- **Immune system**: increases heat shock protein production
- **Sleep and stress**: reduces cortisol, improves sleep quality

## Types of infrared sauna

Three types based on wavelength:

- **Near infrared (NIR)**: penetrates 5mm into skin. Benefits mainly skin and mitochondria.
- **Mid infrared (MIR)**: penetrates 1–2 cm. Best for circulation and joints.
- **Far infrared (FIR)**: penetrates up to 4 cm. The most studied for detox and cardiovascular benefits.

**Full-spectrum saunas** combine all three and are the most complete option.
