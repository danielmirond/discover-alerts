**Red light therapy** (photobiomodulation, PBM) is one of the non-invasive interventions gaining traction in longevity. Specific wavelengths — 630–680 nm (red) and 800–850 nm (near-infrared) — penetrate tissue and **stimulate mitochondrial function** via cytochrome c oxidase. The result: more ATP, less inflammation and better cellular recovery.

## What the evidence says

Recent reviews identify benefits backed by RCTs:

- **Skin**: increased collagen, reduced wrinkles, improved healing
- **Musculoskeletal**: post-exercise recovery, chronic joint pain, tendinopathies
- **Cognition**: preliminary studies show improved cognitive function with transcranial NIR
- **Thyroid**: mixed evidence in Hashimoto's (more research needed)
- **Sleep**: reduced latency if used 1–2h before bed

## Wavelengths — what matters

Not all panels are equal. Wavelengths with evidence:

- **630–660 nm (visible red)**: penetrates 1–2 cm. Optimal for skin and healing
- **810–850 nm (NIR)**: penetrates 3–5 cm. Optimal for muscle, joints and cognition
- **900–1,100 nm**: deeper penetration but more limited human evidence

The best panels offer **both (red + NIR)** in the same panel, with a toggle or simultaneously.

## Usage protocol

Based on Hamblin et al's reviewed studies:

### Dose per zone

- **Distance from panel**: 6–12 in (follow manufacturer specs)
- **Duration**: 10–20 minutes per zone
- **Frequency**: 4–7 days per week
- **Total daily dose**: 15–40 minutes

### Optimal timing

- **Morning**: 5–10 min after waking (mitochondrial activation, alertness)
- **Pre-workout**: 5–10 min (performance)
- **Post-workout**: 10–15 min (recovery)
- **Pre-sleep**: **avoid** — pure red light (660nm) doesn't affect melatonin, but many panels have blue spectrum contamination

### Specific protocols

**Skin**: 10 min face at 8 in, 4–5x/week. Visible results at 8–12 weeks.

**Muscle recovery**: 15 min affected zone at 6 in, post-workout.

**Cognition (transcranial NIR)**: 10 min forehead/temples with NIR only (810–850nm). Preliminary evidence.

## Safety and contraindications

Red light is **very safe** for healthy individuals. Precautions:

- **Protective goggles included** — don't stare directly at LEDs
- **Photosensitizing medication**: consult doctor (some antibiotics, diuretics)
- **Lupus/autoimmune disease**: consult dermatologist
- **Pregnancy**: avoid over abdomen (no studies)
- **Active melanoma**: contraindicated over the lesion
