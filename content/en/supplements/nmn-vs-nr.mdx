## The Race to Boost NAD+

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every living cell. It plays a critical role in cellular energy production, DNA repair and ageing regulation. The problem: **NAD+ levels drop by ~50% between ages 20 and 60**.

This is where NMN and NR come in — two precursors your body converts into NAD+. The question every biohacker asks: which one works better?

## What Is NMN?

**NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide)** is a direct precursor to NAD+. Your body converts it to NAD+ in a single enzymatic step. It was popularised by Dr. David Sinclair of Harvard, who has taken it personally for years.

**Mechanism**: NMN → NAD+ (via NMNAT)

## What Is NR?

**NR (nicotinamide riboside)** is another NAD+ precursor, but it requires two conversion steps: first to NMN, then to NAD+. NR has more human clinical trials thanks to ChromaDex (Tru Niagen).

**Mechanism**: NR → NMN → NAD+ (via NRK → NMNAT)
